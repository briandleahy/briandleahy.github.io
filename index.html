Workflow

My data science work strongly focuses on writing clean, reproducible, modular code that is thoroughly tested. I lean heavily on Python's unittest module for this. The four books that I reach for the most are Martin Fowler's Clean Code, Press et al's Numerical Recipes, Skiena's The Algorithm Design Manual, and Bishop's Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning.

Interests

Currently I'm interested in applying modern machine learning and data science techniques to medical data. My work right now involves using machine learning, especially CNNs, to extract features of interest from the raw data, then combining those extracted features with more traditional methods to gain insight into the underlying processes. I enjoy continuing to learn the machine-learning, the medical practice, and the physiology knowledge that I use daily.

In my spare time, I love to read, cook, hike, run, and hit the gym. In a past life I spent a lot of my time riding and working on old motorcycles, but the premium on space in the Boston area has put that hobby on pause :)
